## Step-by-step guide

Welcome to this step-by-step guide on how to create an application using michelangelo.land.
Each section is accompanied by screenshots to make the steps easier to understand.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1: Log in to michelangelo.land" icon="right-to-bracket">
    1. Visit <a href="https://code.michelangelo.land/sign-in">code.michelangelo.land</a> 👀
    2. **Log in** or register to access the platform 🚀
       You can choose between **two sign-in options**:
       - **Sign in with GitHub**: quickly sign in using your GitHub account.
       - **Continue with Email**: enter your email address and you will receive an **OTP code** via email. Manually enter the code received to confirm your login.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 2: Define your project" icon="code">
    1. After logging in to [code.michelangelo.land](https://code.michelangelo.land) you can start developing right away.
       - **First time login**: when you log in for the first time, michelangelo.land automatically creates your first project, so you can start developing your first app right away without having to set up anything.
       - **Subsequent logins**: the platform will automatically load the last project you worked on, allowing you to pick up where you left off.
    2. **Development interface**
       When you access a project, you will see a screen with the following items:
       - **Development prompts**: you can write prompts to generate components, modify code, or add functionality to your app.
       - **Guide section**: shows the steps to create and test your app.
       - **QR Code**: allows you to view your app directly on your mobile device using Expo Go.
       - **Code section**: displays the project structure with the folder already set up with two files inside: and .

    From here, you can start interacting with michelangelo.land to develop your application. 🚀
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 3: View your App on Mobile" icon="mobile-screen">
    1. After the project is generated, you will see a QR Code in the ‘**Guide**‘ **section**.
    2. **Download** **the** **Expo Go** **app** on your mobile device (if you haven't already done so).
    3. **Scan** **the** **QR Code** with your phone's camera or with the Expo Go app.
    4. The app will open on your device, allowing you to see the **result in real time 📱**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 4: Explore and Edit the code" icon="pen">
    1. In the ‘**Code**‘ **section** you can access the **project structure**.
    2. Navigate through the pre-generated files and folders, including:
       - `_layout.tsx`: the main layout of the app and manages navigation;
       - `index.tsx`: the home page of the app.
    3. Edit the code directly from the platform to **customize your app** via Development Prompt and you can enhance it through the following features:
       - <Icon icon="files" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Select multiple files** of your app at once via the ‘**\+ Add context**’ button. This allows michelangelo.land to have more information and improve the accuracy of the requested changes.
       - <Icon icon="paperclip" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Attach images** via the attachment icon to allow michelangelo.land to replicate a more specific design for your app.
       - <Icon icon="pen" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Manually edit the code** directly in the michelangelo.land editor via the button with the pencil icon.
       - <Icon icon="trash" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Delete files** **via chat** by typing for example "Delete \[file name\]". Alternatively, **right-click** a file in the ‘Code‘ section and select Delete to remove it from the project.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 5: Save and Version the Project on GitHub" icon="github">
    You can **connect your project to a GitHub repository** by following three simple steps:

    1. Click the ‘<Icon icon="github" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Connect**‘ button to connect the project to a repository.
    2. Follow the three steps to complete the connection:
       - **Authorize** michelangelo.land to access your GitHub account.
       - **Install** michelangelo.land in your account.
       - **Select the repository** where you want to save the project.
    3. After connecting, each **change** will be **saved directly** to your repository. This feature allows you to always have access to your project's repository, keeping it versioned and secure.

    You can also **download your app code** directly via the download icon button <Icon icon="arrow-down-to-line" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" />.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 6: Build your App" icon="share">
    With michelangelo.land, you can easily create the Build of your project and publish it by following a few steps.
    1. **Expo account**:<br /> 
        to start the build, you need an Expo account, where the project will be uploaded.
        If you don't have an account yet, create one here: <a href="https://expo.dev/signup">create an Expo account</a>, or <a href="https://expo.dev/login">log in</a> to your existing account.
      
    2. **Project on Expo**:<br />
    after logging in to Expo, you will need to **create a new project** in the Expo Dashboard. This step is essential to build the project developed on code.michelangelo.land.
    
    3. **Build on code.michelangelo.land**:<br /> 
      inside your project on <a href="https://code.michelangelo.land">code.michelangelo.land</a>:
        - <Icon icon="hammer" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> Click the button with the hammer icon.
        - <Icon icon="pen-field" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> A pop-up will appear where you can enter the details of your Expo account and the project you created. Below are the required fields with example values:<br />
          `ID: "3b2a9f88-7d4e-49a5-92c7-a1b23c45d678"`<br />
          `Owner: "my-michelangelo-app"`<br />
          `Slug: "michelangeloproject"`<br />
          `packageName: "com.my-michelangelo-app"`<br />
          `bundleIdentifier: "com.my-michelangelo-app"`<br />
          `Token: "zxywvu9876543210abcdefghijklmnopqrstuv"`<br />
        - After filling in the required fields, click on the 'Build Project' button.
    4. **Build Status**:<br />
      After starting, the Build will have three states:
        - <Icon icon="clock" iconType="light" color="#fc9020" size="20" /> _In Queue_
        - <Icon icon="spinner" iconType="regular" color="#1b87d9" size="20" /> _In Progress_
        - <Icon icon="check" iconType="regular" color="#4bb61c" size="20" /> _Success_ (successfully completed) or <Icon icon="xmark" iconType="regular" color="#e20b0b" size="20" /> _Failed_ (error during the Build).<br />
          If the Build fails, you can contact <a href="https://docs.michelangelo.land/introduction#support">michelangelo.land support</a> for assistance.
    5. **Completed Build**:<br />
      When the Build completes successfully, you will receive a direct <a>link to the Build</a> in your Expo account. Here you can:
        - **Check the status of the Build** (if it was successful, if it is still in progress or if it failed).
        - **Distribute your app** to TestFlight (iOS) or Play Store (Android).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Conclusion" icon="party-horn">
    You just created your application with michelangelo.land! 🥳 Now you can continue to modify, improve and distribute it easily.

    If you need help, visit the <a href="/introduction#support">Support section</a> of the documentation or <a href="https://docs.michelangelo.land/introduction#stay-connected">join the Community!</a>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
