## Step-by-step guide

Welcome to this step-by-step guide on how to create an application using Michelangelo.land.
Each section will make the steps easier to understand.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step 1: Log in to Michelangelo.land" icon="right-to-bracket">
    1. Visit <a href="https://michelangelo.land/sign-in">Michelangelo.land</a> 👀
    2. **Log in** or register to access the platform 🚀.
       You can choose between **three sign-in options**:
       - <Icon icon="google" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Sign in with Google**: sign in using your Google account.
       - <Icon icon="github" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Sign in with GitHub**: quickly sign in using your GitHub account.
       - <Icon icon="envelope" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Continue with Email**: enter your email address and you will receive an **OTP code** via email. Manually enter the code received to confirm your login.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 2: Create your project" icon="code">
    1. After logging in to [Michelangelo.land](https://michelangelo.land) you can **start developing** right away: you'll see a **text area** where you can provide a short **description of your app**. Once submitted, you'll be taken to the development interface, ready to continue building.<br />
    
        Alternatively, you can create a new project using **another method**:
        - <Icon icon="folder-open" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> Click the folder icon in the right sidebar menu to access the ‘**Projects**‘ **section**.
        - Click the **‘New Project‘ button** in the top left corner. 
        - A pop-up will appear, allowing you to **enter a name for your project**.<br />

        Either way, the development environment will open immediately, ready for you to use.

        In the ‘**Projects**‘ section, you can manage your existing projects. Each project card displays its name, ID, and last edit time. From here, you can:
        - Open a project by clicking the ‘**Open**’ button.
        - <Icon icon="trash" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" />  ’**Delete**’ a project by clicking the button with the trash bin icon. When you click the delete icon, a pop-up will appear asking you to enter the project name to confirm the deletion. You can then:
          - Click ‘**Delete Project**’ to permanently remove the project (this action is irreversible).
          - Click ‘**Cancel**’ to go back without deleting the project.
    
    2. **Development interface**<br />
       When you access a project, you will see a screen with the following items:
       - **Development prompts**: you can write prompts to generate your app, components, modify code, or add functionality to your app.
       - **‘Preview‘ section**: shows the steps to test your app, including an online preview and a QR Code for <a href="https://expo.dev/go">Expo Go</a>.
       - **‘Code‘ section**: displays the project structure with the `app` folder already set up with two files inside: `_layout.tsx` and `index.tsx`, and more features.

    From here, you can continue interacting with Michelangelo.land to develop your application. 🚀
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 3: View your App" icon="mobile-screen">
    1. After the project is generated, you will see a QR Code and an online preview in the ‘**Preview**‘ **section**.  
    2. You can view your app in two ways:  
     - **Via Expo Go**:  
       - **Download** the <a href="https://expo.dev/go">**Expo Go**</a> app on your mobile device (if you haven't already).  
       - **Scan** the **QR Code** with your phone's camera (for IOS) or with the Expo Go app (for Android).  
       - The app will open on your device, allowing you to see the **result in real time 📱**.  
     - **Via Online Preview**:  
       - Click on the ‘**Preview**‘ section to see your app directly in your browser. 
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 4: Explore and Edit the code" icon="pen">
    1. In the ‘**Code**‘ **section** you can access the **project structure**.
    2. Navigate through the pre-generated `app` folder and files, including:
       - `_layout.tsx`: the main layout of the app and manages navigation;
       - `index.tsx`: the home page of the app.
    3. Edit the code directly from the platform to **customize your app** via Development Prompt and you can enhance it through the following features:
       - <Icon icon="files" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Select multiple files** of your app at once via the ‘**\+ Add context**’ button. This allows michelangelo.land to have more information and improve the accuracy of the requested changes.
       - <Icon icon="paperclip" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Attach images** via the attachment icon to allow Michelangelo.land to replicate a more specific design for your app.
       - <Icon icon="pen" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Manually edit the code** directly in the Michelangelo.land editor via the button with the pencil icon.
       - <Icon icon="trash" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Delete files** **via chat** by typing for example "Delete \[file name\]". Alternatively, **right-click** a file in the ‘Code‘ section and select Delete to remove it from the project.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 5: Save and Version the Project on GitHub" icon="github">
    You can **connect your project to a GitHub repository** by following three simple steps:

    1. Click the ‘<Icon icon="github" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Connect**‘ button to connect the project to a repository.
    2. Follow the three steps to complete the connection:
       - **Authorize** Michelangelo.land to access your GitHub account.
       - **Install** Michelangelo.land in your account.
       - **Select the repository** where you want to save the project.
    3. After connecting, each **change** will be **saved directly** to your repository. This feature allows you to always have access to your project's repository, keeping it versioned and secure.

    You can also **download your app code** directly via the download icon button <Icon icon="arrow-down-to-line" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" />.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 6: Share Preview" icon="eye">
    Want to show your app before the official deployment? Michelangelo.land lets you **share a live preview of your project** with a simple public link. <br />
    1. Click on the ‘<Icon icon="arrow-up-from-bracket" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **Publish**‘ button in the top-right corner of interface and a popover will open with publish options.
    2. Locate the **Share Preview** section inside the popover.
    3. Toggle the **switch** to enable or disable the preview link:
        - <Icon icon="eye" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **enable** will make the link active and **visible to the public**.
        - <Icon icon="eye-slash" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> **disable** will make the link inactive and **not visible to the public**.
    4. Click the <Icon icon="copy" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> clipboard icon to **copy the link**.
    5. **Share the link** with anyone — no login or Michelangelo account required to access the preview.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Step 7: Deploy your App" icon="arrow-up-from-bracket">
    With Michelangelo.land you can easily distribute your app by following a few steps. The Deploy process allows you to publish your application on **TestFlight (iOS)** and **Google Play (Android)**.
    1. **Expo and Stores account**:<br /> 
       - to get started, make sure you have an Expo account. If you don't have one, you can create one here: <a href="https://expo.dev/signup">create an Expo account</a>, or <a href="https://expo.dev/login">log in</a> to your existing account.
       - In addition, to distribute your app on TestFlight (iOS) or Google Play (Android), you'll also need the corresponding **developer accounts for each store**. <br />
         You can find all the details on how to set them up in Expo’s official guide: <a href="https://docs.expo.dev/build/setup/#build-for-app-stores">Build for app stores – Expo Docs</a>
      
    2. **Project on Expo**:<br />
    after logging in to Expo, access the Expo Dashboard and **create a new project**. You will need this to connect Michelangelo.land to your Expo environment and start the Deploy.

    3. **Deploy from Michelangelo.land**:<br /> 
      inside your project on <a href="https://michelangelo.land">Michelangelo.land</a>:
        - <Icon icon="rocket-launch" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> Click on the ‘**Deploy**‘ button (with the rocket icon).
        - <Icon icon="pen-field" iconType="regular" color="#7c7c7c" size="20" /> A popup will open, divided into **4 steps** (the values ​​of the fields below are for example):<br />
           1. Enter:<br />
               • `App Name` (your app name)<br />
               • `App Icon` (your app icon)<br />
               Click ‘Next‘
          2. Enter the Expo project data:<br />
              `ID: "3b2a9f88-7d4e-49a5-92c7-a1b23c45d678"`<br />
              `Owner: "my-michelangelo-app"`<br />
              `Slug: "michelangeloproject"`<br />
              (These data are visible in the Expo Dashboard)<br />
              Click ‘Next‘
           3. Enter your Expo Access Token:<br />
              `Token: "zxywvu9876543210abcdefghijklmnopqrstuv"`<br />
              (Always recoverable from the Expo project Dashboard)<br />
              Click ‘Next‘
           4. Fill in the Store fields according to your needs:<br />
              `iOS Bundle Identifier: "1234567890"` (from TestFlight)<br />
              `Apple App Store Connect App ID: "123456789"` (from Apple App Store Connect)<br />
              `Android Package Name: "ex.com.myapp"` (from Google Play Console)<br />
          
        - After filling in the required fields, click on the ‘**Submit**‘ button.
    4. **Deployment Status**:<br />
      After submitting your data:
        - You will see a message with <Icon icon="rocket-launch" iconType="light" color="#fc9020" size="20" /> icon: <br />
           **"Your app is preparing for launch..."**
        - The Deploy button will show a dynamic status:<br />
           <Icon icon="clock" iconType="light" color="#fc9020" size="20" /> _Queue_ <br />
           <Icon icon="spinner" iconType="regular" color="#1b87d9" size="20" /> _In Progress_ <br />
           <Icon icon="check" iconType="regular" color="#4bb61c" size="20" /> _Deploy_ (green border) or <Icon icon="xmark" iconType="regular" color="#e20b0b" size="20" /> _Failed_ (red border).<br />
          
          In case of error (**_Failed_**), you can **repeat the procedure** by clicking the ‘Deploy‘ button again. The previous data will have to be re-entered for greater security.<br />
          Also, you can contact <a href="https://docs.michelangelo.land/introduction#support">Michelangelo.land support</a> for assistance.
    5. **Distributed App**:<br />
        When the status is **_Deploy_** (green border), the Deployment will be completed successfully:
        - in the popup (by clicking on ‘Deploy‘ (green border)) you will find a **direct link to your Expo dashboard** where you can **view the status of your Build and Submit** (submitting the app to the stores).<br />
        
        Once all the processes have been successfully completed, **your app will be on TestFlight and Google Play**, ready to be shared.
  </Accordion>

    You just created your application with Michelangelo.land! 🥳 Now you can continue to modify, improve and distribute it easily.
